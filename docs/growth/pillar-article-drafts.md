# Pillar Draft 1
## Fractional CTO in Australia: Operating Model for 10–100 Person Teams

### SEO target
- Primary: fractional cto australia
- Secondary: cto advisory services, startup cto advisor australia

### Draft

Most teams don’t need more technology ideas. They need better technical decisions executed at the right pace.

For Australian growth-stage businesses, fractional CTO support is usually strongest when three conditions are present:
1) delivery confidence is dropping,
2) architecture decisions are compounding risk,
3) full-time executive overhead is premature.

A practical fractional CTO model should deliver three things quickly: clearer priorities, tighter governance, and measurable progress.

## What a strong engagement looks like

### 1) Decision architecture
- clear decision rights (who decides what)
- explicit trade-off rules (speed vs risk vs cost)
- weekly escalation cadence

### 2) Delivery operating rhythm
- one weekly leadership delivery review
- one risk register with real owners
- one roadmap with ranked priorities and dependency visibility

### 3) Technical risk control
- architecture guardrails for new work
- debt triage based on business risk
- rollback expectations for high-impact changes

## Typical 30–90 day trajectory

- Days 1–14: baseline delivery and risk map
- Days 15–45: intervention rollout on highest-leverage bottlenecks
- Days 46–90: operating cadence hardening and leadership handover

## Who this is for
- founder-led teams from 10 to 100 people
- product organisations under delivery pressure
- businesses scaling without stable technical governance

## Who this is not for
- teams looking only for hands-on coding capacity
- businesses unwilling to enforce delivery governance
- organisations wanting strategy without execution ownership

## What to measure
- replan rate
- blocked-time ratio
- decision latency
- release predictability
- incident rework trend

Fractional CTO support is not a title substitute. It is an operating-system intervention for teams that need better outcomes without permanent executive cost too early.

# Pillar Draft 2
## AI Consulting for Startups: From Pilot to Measurable ROI

### SEO target
- Primary: ai consulting for startups
- Secondary: ai integration consulting australia, ai roadmap for startups

### Draft

Most AI pilots fail for one simple reason: they’re selected for novelty, not outcomes.

For startups and growth-stage teams, practical AI consulting should do one job first: identify where AI changes business performance in measurable ways.

## Start with one bottleneck, not a roadmap deck

High-signal first use-cases usually sit in repetitive, decision-heavy workflows:
- quoting and pricing support
- support triage and resolution workflows
- internal knowledge retrieval for ops teams
- structured content generation with review controls

## A practical prioritisation model
Score each candidate use-case against:
1) business impact,
2) implementation feasibility,
3) risk-adjusted time-to-value.

If it doesn’t score across all three, it is not phase one.

## Pilot-to-production structure

### Phase 1 (2–4 weeks): Baseline
- current process metrics
- data readiness check
- risk and compliance constraints

### Phase 2 (4–8 weeks): Controlled pilot
- narrow scope
- clear owner
- quality and fallback guardrails

### Phase 3 (8–12 weeks): Production decision
- performance vs baseline
- operating cost vs value
- rollout, rework, or retire decision

## Common failure modes to avoid
- broad “AI strategy” with no sequencing logic
- multiple pilots with no accountable owner
- quality controls added late
- no explicit retirement criteria

## What to measure
- cycle time reduction
- error/rework reduction
- throughput uplift
- cost-to-serve impact
- customer or team experience impact

A good AI consulting engagement reduces uncertainty, not just experimentation time.
