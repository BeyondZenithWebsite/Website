Signals you likely need fractional CTO support

  • Roadmaps are committed, then repeatedly re-planned
  • Ownership is blurred between product, engineering, and leadership
  • Architecture trade-offs stall and create recurring delivery drag
  • AI initiatives stay in pilot mode without measurable business impact

What changes during an engagement

We install a weekly delivery operating rhythm with explicit risk visibility, faster architecture decisions, and tighter sequencing by business impact. The goal is fewer surprises, clearer accountability, and measurable recovery momentum.

Fast fit check (2-minute decision aid)

If 2 or more of the following are true, a fractional CTO sprint is usually the fastest path to de-risk the next quarter:

  • Two or more roadmap commitments slipped in the last 90 days.
  • Critical architecture decisions stay unresolved for more than 2 weeks.
  • Delivery blockers escalate repeatedly without clear owner accountability.
  • Leadership confidence in engineering forecasts is currently low.

Bring these four answers to the strategy call and we can map a practical first 14-day intervention.

What you get in the first 14 days

  • Delivery baseline: planned vs shipped reliability, dependency drag, and current blocker map.
  • Risk register: top execution and architecture risks ranked by commercial impact.
  • Decision protocol: clear decision owners, escalation path, and weekly review cadence.
  • 30-day intervention plan: focused recovery sequence with named owners and expected outcomes.

This gives leadership a decision-ready view before committing to a longer retainer.

Diagnostic questions to start with

  • What percentage of committed work ships on time?
  • Which dependencies create the most release delays?
  • How long do major technical decisions remain unresolved?
  • What delivery risks are escalated to leadership every week?

Recovery model

Baseline → Intervention → Result. First establish delivery truth, then apply targeted operating and architecture interventions, then track measurable outcomes over a 30-90 day window.

FAQ

When should we engage a fractional CTO instead of hiring full-time?

If delivery risk is immediate and you need senior technical leadership within weeks, fractional CTO support is usually faster and lower-risk than a full-time executive hire process.

How quickly can we see measurable delivery improvement?

Most teams see leading indicators within 30 days (risk visibility, decision throughput, weekly cadence) and stronger delivery predictability within 60–90 days.

Do you work with existing engineering leaders?

Yes. Engagements are designed to strengthen internal leaders, improve decision quality, and transfer a sustainable operating rhythm into the existing team.
