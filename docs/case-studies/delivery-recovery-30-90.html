Detailed case narrative: logistics SaaS delivery recovery

Context: The company was a scale-up logistics SaaS provider with a 22-engineer product and platform team. Leadership had strong market demand but weak delivery confidence. Major customer requests were delayed, and internal trust between product and engineering was deteriorating.

Initial problems: Release planning slipped repeatedly because dependencies were hidden until late in the cycle. Architecture complexity increased integration risk, and incident response consumed capacity intended for roadmap work. Product priorities changed frequently without explicit trade-off decisions.

Diagnostic findings: Baseline review identified four systemic constraints: replan frequency above target, unclear ownership of cross-team dependencies, high variance in cycle time for priority work, and limited visibility of architecture risk in weekly planning forums. Leadership was making decisions with incomplete execution data.

Interventions implemented: We introduced a weekly delivery governance model with explicit risk review and escalation. Roadmap sequencing was adjusted around dependency risk and business impact. Architecture simplification focused on two high-friction service boundaries that caused repeated release contention. Teams implemented WIP limits and clearer ownership for integration tasks.

Measurable outcomes: Within 45 days, planning confidence improved and release cadence shifted from quarterly batching toward biweekly increments for high-priority streams. Incident-driven rework dropped materially, and leadership could make faster decisions using a concise operating dashboard. Customer-facing delivery reliability improved and internal forecast quality increased.

Why this mattered: The engagement did not rely on a full rewrite or large headcount increase. It restored delivery predictability through operating discipline, architecture pragmatism, and explicit decision pathways. This pattern is repeatable for companies facing similar software project rescue conditions.

How to apply this case to your environment

If your team faces engineering delivery problems, start by quantifying risk and identifying the top two constraints that repeatedly delay outcomes. Pair this with a clear intervention sequence and weekly decision cadence. Use the Delivery Recovery Playbook and CTO Delivery Scorecard to establish baseline clarity before execution changes.
